Dumbbell Bench Press

← Back to Chest Workouts

Overview

The Dumbbell Bench Press is a fundamental chest-building exercise that targets the pectorals, triceps, and shoulders. It provides a greater range of motion than the barbell press and helps correct muscle imbalances.

Muscles Worked

  • Pectoralis Major (Chest)
  • Anterior Deltoids (Shoulders)
  • Triceps Brachii

How to Perform

  1. Lie on a flat bench with a dumbbell in each hand resting on your thighs.
  2. Use your thighs to help raise the dumbbells as you lie back on the bench.
  3. Hold the dumbbells shoulder-width apart with palms facing forward.
  4. Press the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended.
  5. Slowly lower the dumbbells back to chest level.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips

  • Keep your feet flat on the floor for stability.
  • Control the movement – don’t let the dumbbells drop quickly.
  • Keep your back flat and avoid arching excessively.
