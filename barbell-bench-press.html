Barbell Bench Press

Muscles Targeted

The Barbell Bench Press primarily targets the pectoralis major (chest muscles), but it also engages the anterior deltoids (shoulders) and triceps brachii (arms).

How to Perform

  1. Lie flat on a bench with your feet firmly planted on the floor.
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  3. Unrack the bar and hold it straight over your chest with arms fully extended.
  4. Slowly lower the bar to your mid-chest while keeping elbows at about 75 degrees.
  5. Pause briefly, then press the bar back to the starting position by extending your arms.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips

  • Keep your back slightly arched and shoulder blades retracted during the lift.
  • Don't bounce the bar off your chest — maintain control at all times.
  • Use a spotter for safety, especially when lifting heavy weights.

Common Mistakes

  • Flaring elbows out too far to the sides — increases risk of shoulder injury.
  • Not using full range of motion.
  • Lifting hips off the bench during the press.
